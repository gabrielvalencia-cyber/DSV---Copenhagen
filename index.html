01 · AI & technology transformation Transform the business with AI — customs is a named flagship

DSV targets ~DKK 9bn of productivity from AI and technology by 2030, called out “particularly in customs and booking processes.” Reinforced by the 2025 Annual Report’s “AI Factory” and a named Customs AI initiative. Active now.

02 · Schenker integration & synergies Complete the Schenker integration and capture the synergies

The ~DKK 15.9bn DB Schenker acquisition made DSV the world’s largest freight forwarder (~147,000 staff, 90+ countries). Integration ~45% done by Q1 2026, targeted ~70% by end-2026; full ~DKK 9bn synergies from 2027.

03 · Operational excellence & margin Operational excellence and margin discipline

2030 targets of ~45% group conversion ratio and ~20% ROIC; SG&A already pulled to ~14.1% of revenue in 2025, with nearly all Schenker customers retained. Do more, at lower cost, without losing service.

Which customs work is still manual, inconsistent, or slow — and shouldn’t be?

— Extraction & AbstractionCustoms document intake & data quality Rank

Commercial invoices, packing lists, and certificates of origin arrive in inconsistent formats across clients and regions. Operators re-key data by hand — creating errors, rework, and inconsistent records before clearance. Felt by customs operators, and the clients whose shipments get held.

The opportunityExtract, normalise, and validate document data against master data before clearance, with human-in-the-loop review.
— Reporting & ObservabilityProcess & SOP harmonisation across the merged org and 5 regions Rank

Post-Schenker, two organisations carry different procedures, document standards, and tribal knowledge across five regions. Onboarding is slow and execution inconsistent. Felt by regional customs leaders and new joiners.

The opportunityCapture and standardise SOPs and surface the right procedure per country on demand — with citations and version control.
— Extraction & AbstractionHS tariff classification consistency Rank

Classification varies by broker and region, driving compliance risk and duty leakage. The same product can be coded differently across the network. Felt by compliance teams and clients exposed to penalties or overpayment.

The opportunityAI-assisted classification against a coded catalogue with a full audit trail, consistent across regions.
— Reporting & ObservabilityRegulatory change monitoring & client guidance Rank

IEEPA refunds, the CBAM definitive period, and shifting tariffs move fast across jurisdictions. Clients ask “what do we do now,” and answers must be fast and consistent. Felt by trade-compliance leads and account teams.

The opportunityMonitor rule changes, flag affected entries and clients, and auto-draft client-facing guidance.
— Automation & AgentsEntry validation & pre-clearance exception handling Rank

Errors caught at the border cause holds and delays, with limited upfront visibility into exposure. Felt by operators firefighting exceptions and clients facing disruption.

The opportunityPre-screen entries for completeness and risk, route exceptions to humans, and reduce border holds.
— Automation & AgentsDuty refund readiness & recovery (IEEPA) Rank

Reviewing historical entries for refund eligibility at scale is hard when data is scattered across systems. Felt by clients with exposure and the brokers supporting recovery.

The opportunityReview entries at scale, identify recoverable duties, and assemble the supporting documentation.
